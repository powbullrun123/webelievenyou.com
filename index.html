The Hebrew Word for Truth

What is אמת (EMET)?

א Aleph First letter of the alphabet The Beginning
מ Mem Middle letter of the alphabet The Journey
ת Tav Last letter of the alphabet The Fulfillment

EMET is composed of the first, middle, and last letters of the Hebrew alphabet — read right to left. Truth spans everything, from beginning to end.

Truth From Beginning to End

Remove the first letter (Aleph) from EMET and you get MET — which means "death" in Hebrew. Without God at the beginning, truth dies. EMET teaches us that God must be at the foundation of all truth.

"You will know the truth, and the truth shall set you free."

— John 8:32

The Law Given to Moses

God gave Moses these commandments on Mount Sinai — not as chains, but as the path to freedom.

Our Duty to God
IYou shall have no other gods before Me.
IIYou shall not make for yourself an idol or worship any graven image.
IIIYou shall not take the name of the LORD your God in vain.
IVRemember the Sabbath day, to keep it holy.
VHonor your father and your mother.
Our Duty to Each Other
VIYou shall not murder.
VIIYou shall not commit adultery.
VIIIYou shall not steal.
IXYou shall not bear false witness against your neighbor.
XYou shall not covet anything that belongs to your neighbor.

"If you love Me, keep My commandments."

— John 14:15

These are not rules to oppress us. They are the blueprint for peace — the same peace Jesus lived and taught. When we follow them, we build God's Kingdom right here, right now.

Living Like Jesus

The Way to Discover Heaven on Earth

01

Love Without Condition

Jesus loved the outcast, the broken, and the lost. True peace begins when we love others exactly as they are.

"A new command I give you: Love one another." — John 13:34

02

Forgive and Be Free

Confession and forgiveness break every chain. When we let go of anger, we make room for God's grace.

"If we confess our sins, He is faithful and just to forgive us." — 1 John 1:9

03

Serve One Another

Jesus washed the feet of His disciples. The kingdom is built by humility, by lifting others up.

"The greatest among you shall be your servant." — Matthew 23:11
